About the practices

The practices presented are the result of the review of different game design methods, models and frameworks. A process of characterization of the most used practices was carried out in order to consider them as best practices in game design.

The solution includes the representation of the following game design practices characterized:

  1. Goal-oriented requirements specification: Guides the way of working for a correct specification of the requirements according to the goals that the stakeholder wants to achieve. It works from the moment they are conceived, until they are coherent.

  2. Formal design of the game world: Guides the way of working for an adequate definition of the world in which the game takes place.

  3. Incremental design of the rules of the game: Guides the way of working to establish the rules of the game.

  4. Audiovisual design of the game style: Guides the way of working for the correct design of the visual and sound style of the game.

  5. Evolutionary design of game interfaces: Guides the way of working that allows the design of game interfaces.

  6. Overall definition of the game architecture: Guides the way of working that allows a correct definition of the architecture proposed for the game.

  7. Prototype-based game construction: Guides in the evaluation of the design process for its subsequent implementation, through the construction of prototypes.

The design of the solution, as stated by Henao Roqueme (2018), is carried out according to the requirements of the stakeholder, therefore, it is important to develop states of the practice "Goal-oriented requirements specification" to be successful in the design.

The percentage of game progress upon completion of the proposed practice is relative for each game, and depends on the characteristics of the game to be designed.
